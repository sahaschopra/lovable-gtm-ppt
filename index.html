Phase 01 · Position

Three things you have to nail.

01

Pick your ICP

Be specific. Not “small businesses” — but “remote agencies doing $250K–$10M ARR.”

If your ICP fits everyone, it converts no one.

02

Define the real problem

Revenue impact — not surface symptom. “Productivity” = cost center. “Scale revenue without scaling headcount” = growth lever.

Same product. Different framing. Wildly different price.

03

Differentiate

Map the competitive landscape. Find the gap nobody else owns.

Tools: Visualping · Price2Spy · G2 · Capterra · Claude / ChatGPT for synthesis

PHASE 02

VALIDATE

“Does anyone actually want this?”

Phase 2 chapter. Position is set — now you find out if anyone cares.

Two Paths to Validation

Find your earliest signal.

PATH A · FAST

Run paid ads.

Test 3–4 positioning angles with small budgets. The angle that converts is the pain your ICP cares about.

  • Signal in days, not months
  • Tests the messaging directly
  • Costs money up front
PATH B · COMPOUNDING

Build in public.

Show up daily. Share your build. The obsessed will find you — and bring others.

  • Lower CAC over time
  • Compounds for months
  • Slower to start

Best founders do both. Paid for speed. Organic for depth.

Don't frame as either/or. The point is paid buys speed, organic buys compounding — they reinforce each other.

The Validation Truth

Polite interest is not signal.

1%
Your top 1% of users — your obsessed core — will drive most of your growth. Find them. Obsess over them.
Power Law · Peter Thiel, “Zero to One”
Obsessive use Daily, without prompts or reminders
Unprompted sharing Telling friends, posting screenshots
Building on top Templates, hacks, customizations

Sean Ellis Test: if 40%+ of users say they’d be “very disappointed” to lose your product, that’s PMF.

Lead with Power Law — that's the operating principle. Three traits show what obsession looks like. Sean Ellis 40% test is the measurable bar at the bottom.

PHASE 03

CONVERT

“Will they pay for it?”

Phase 3 chapter. They want it — now will they pay for it? This is where most startups die (the chasm we just saw).

Phase 03 · Convert

Sell early. Price by behavior.

PRINCIPLE 01

Sell before it’s ready.

Pre-sales. Paid pilots. Founding-member tiers. Money is the only validation that scales — a “yes” without a credit card is a maybe.

  • Revenue from day one
  • Real feedback, not polite nods
  • Filters tire-kickers fast
PRINCIPLE 02

Price by behavior, not feature.

Early adopters get founding pricing locked in. In return: feedback, testimonials, referrals. Discount as a deal — not as weakness.

  • Discount = signal, not surrender
  • Locks in your obsessed users
  • Funds the next 10 features

Don’t wait for perfect. Charge early. Listen. Iterate.

Both principles reinforce each other. Founders get squeamish about charging early — push them to do it anyway. The credit-card test is the punchline.

The Aha Moment

Find the one behavior that predicts who pays.

Slack 2,000 messages

sent within a single team — the moment a team became sticky.

Facebook 7 friends in 10 days

the threshold that predicted long-term retention better than anything else.

Dropbox 1st file uploaded

into a shared folder — Drew Houston’s “Magic Moment” that hooked retention.

Your homework: what’s the one thing your obsessed users do in the first 5 minutes that nobody else does? Build for that.

Three category-defining startups, three different micro-signals. Each is specific, early, and predictive. Walk through them, then turn the question on the room: what’s yours?

PHASE 04

COMPOUND

“How do I scale without burning out?”

Final phase. They’re paying. Now: how do you make every dollar in compound? Loops, content, customers as distribution.

Phase 04 · Compound

Build Loops.
Not Funnels.

FUNNEL

“One-way pipe.”

  • Customer → product → exit
  • Each acquisition costs the same
  • Stop spending — growth stops
  • Linear, never compounding
→
LOOP

“Each customer brings more.”

  • Customer → product → invites others
  • Notion templates: users build & share
  • CAC drops as the loop spins
  • Compounds, even when you sleep
Metcalfe’s Law

Every new user makes the product more valuable for every other user. That’s a loop.

The funnel/loop framing is the conceptual unlock for Phase 4. Notion’s template gallery is the canonical example — users build templates, share them, new users copy the templates and sign up. Self-feeding. End on Metcalfe.

Phase 04 · Compound

Make once. Distribute everywhere. Compound forever.

01 · Make
One core piece Long-form video · Essay · Podcast
↓
02 · Distribute
SEO AEO GEO Short-form clips LinkedIn posts Newsletter

03 · Compound
Compounds for months — while paid stops the day you stop paying.

Lindy Effect The longer a piece of content has worked, the longer it’s likely to keep working.

Content calendar = posting on schedule and hoping. Content engine = make one core piece, then distribute it across every surface where your ICP shows up. Paid stops when you stop paying. Content keeps working while you sleep. End on Lindy.

Case Study · Notion · Deep Dive #2

Your customers will sell it for you —
if it’s worth selling.

$10B Valuation
30M+ Users
1B+ #notion views on TikTok

Students, creators, and productivity influencers built entire audiences around Notion templates. Notion didn’t pay for any of it. The product was the marketing. The community was the sales team.

The Mechanism

Templates are shareable. Shareable creates audiences. Audiences drive sign-ups. Each user becomes a node in your distribution.

Notion didn’t buy growth — it earned it by making the product itself shareable. Templates are the artifact. The artifact is the loop. Connect this back to slide 24: this IS the loop. Then pivot to: most founders can’t even measure if their content is doing this.

The Hard Truth

You can’t compound what you can’t measure.

WHAT MOST FOUNDERS SEE

“Posts went up.
Signups went up.”

  • Vanity metrics — impressions, likes, signups
  • No connection between content & revenue
  • Can’t tell what to double down on
  • Burning cash on what feels right
→
WHAT ACTUALLY MATTERS

“Which post drove
which dollar?”

  • Content → signup → activation → revenue
  • Real attribution across every channel
  • Know exactly where to invest the next $1
  • Compound what works. Cut what doesn’t.

Without attribution, every other phase is a guess.
